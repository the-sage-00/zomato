The Problem
India's CPGRAMS (Centralized Public Grievance Redress and Monitoring System) receives over 25 lakh
grievances every year across 90+ central ministries. The average resolution time is 45–60 days. Nearly 30% of
complaints are misrouted to the wrong department entirely — restarting the clock from zero. Citizens file in 22
languages, but officers respond only in Hindi or English, leaving crores of rural and semi-urban citizens
completely in the dark about the status of their own complaint.
The core failures are:
No intelligent routing — complaints are manually sorted by clerks; wrong-department filing wastes
weeks
Zero NLP processing — identical complaints are treated as unique every time, no pattern detection
Language wall — a Tamil-speaking farmer in Thanjavur cannot understand a Hindi response about his
land records
No accountability loop — citizens have no way to know why their complaint was closed, or appeal
meaningfully
Duplicate flooding — the same systemic issue (e.g., pension delay in one district) gets filed 5,000 times
individually, clogging the system with no escalation to the root cause
This is not a resource problem. It is a software intelligence problem.
The Solution — NyayaAI
A full-stack AI platform that triages, routes, resolves, and learns from citizen grievances — in any Indian
language.
NyayaAI sits on top of CPGRAMS as an intelligent middleware layer. It does not replace the government
system — it makes it dramatically smarter. Every complaint that enters gets processed through a five-stage AI
pipeline before a single human officer touches it.
How It Works (5-Stage Pipeline)
Stage 1 — Multilingual Ingestion
Citizens submit via web, mobile app, WhatsApp bot, or IVR voice call. IndicBERT + IndicTrans2 (AI4Bharat)
transcribes and translates all 22 scheduled languages into a canonical English representation, preserving intent
and emotion tags.
Stage 2 — Intelligent Classification & Routing
A fine-tuned BERT classifier trained on 3 years of CPGRAMS historical data assigns the complaint to the
correct ministry, department, and officer tier with 94%+ accuracy — eliminating manual misrouting entirely.
Stage 3 — Duplicate & Pattern Clustering
FAISS-powered semantic similarity search detects near-duplicate complaints in real time. When 50+ similar
complaints emerge from the same geography within 7 days, the system auto-generates a systemic issue alert —
escalating directly to the district collector or ministry secretary rather than individual officers.
Stage 4 — AI-Assisted Draft Resolution
Officers receive a pre-drafted response generated by a fine-tuned LLM, citing relevant government circulars,
RTI provisions, and past resolution precedents. Officers approve, modify, or override — they are never
bypassed. This cuts average officer response drafting time from 40 minutes to under 5 minutes.
Stage 5 — Multilingual Citizen Notification
The final resolution is delivered back to the citizen in their original language — SMS, WhatsApp, or voice call.
A plain-language explanation replaces bureaucratic jargon. Citizens can rate resolution quality and flag
unsatisfactory closures for Level-2 review.
