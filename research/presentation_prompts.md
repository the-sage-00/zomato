## Page 1: The Core Metrics (4 Boxes)

### Box 1: Corrected Dwell Time (Text)
**Headline:** Tier 1: Corrected Dwell Time (Zero Cost)
**Content:**
*   **The Problem:** The total time a rider spends near a restaurant is messy. Like timing a trip to a store, it includes finding parking, walking in, waiting, and walking back out.
*   **The Solution:** We only care about the time spent staring at the chef. We mathematically slice out the approach, parking, and walking time.
*   **The Impact:** We find the *pure* kitchen wait time. It's totally automatic, free, and nobody can fake it.

### Box 3: Smart FOR Validation (Text)
**Headline:** Smart FOR Validation
**Content:**
*   **The Reality:** The "Food Ready" button is like the boy who cried wolf. Many chefs press it early when the food isn't actually ready yet. Over 70% of these early clicks are lies!
*   **The Fix:** We give every restaurant a "Trust Score" (0-100), just like grading a test.
*   **The Result:** Our system learns to only listen to the honest chefs and completely ignores the buttons pressed by the fibbers.

## Page 2: Contextual & Leading Indicators (6 Boxes)

### Box 1: Merchant Behavior (Text)
**Headline:** Tier 2: Merchant Behavior (Low Friction)
**Content:**
*   **The Concept:** When you are given too much homework, you write slower and get messy. Kitchens are the same!
*   **The Metric:** We watch how long it takes a chef to acknowledge an order on their tablet, and exactly how fast their fingers tap the screen. 
*   **The Logic:** An overwhelmed, super-busy kitchen doesn't have time to tap the tablet fast. Slow, delayed taps mean a flooded kitchen.

### Box 3: AKAI - Ambient Kitchen AI (Text)
**Headline:** AKAI: Ambient Kitchen AI
**Content:**
*   **The Concept:** A tiny, smart listening brain that lives inside the restaurant's order tablet.
*   **The Superpower:** It simply listens to the noise in the room (like pots clanging and voices yelling) to know if it's busy, without taking pictures or recording words.
*   **The Advantage:** It's an early warning alarm. It hears the chaos *before* a delivery rider is even asked to start driving there.

### Box 5: External Busyness (Text)
**Headline:** Tier 3: External Busyness (Context)
**Content:**
*   **The Concept:** Imagine counting how many people walk into a pizza place to guess how busy the oven is. We use Google to do this for us.
*   **The Value:** We get totally free data showing us real crowds of people walking around the restaurant.
*   **The Use Case:** It acts as a helpful clue. Even if there are no Zomato orders, if there's a huge crowd eating inside the restaurant, we know the kitchen is already super busy!
