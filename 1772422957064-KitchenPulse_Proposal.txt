THE PROBLEM — ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
The Silence & Penalty Cycle
The KPT model is only as accurate as its inputs. The primary input — merchant-marked FOR — is systematically
corrupted by a behavioural incentive problem: merchants are penalised for being late, so they mark food ready before
it is. This Fast-Tapping affects ~35% of orders and feeds false data into the model.

INSIGHT 1 — PASSIVE KITCHEN LOAD INTELLIGENCE
IoT Device Detection + Rider Login Classification
A low-cost Bluetooth/WiFi probe installed at the restaurant passively detects all nearby mobile devices. Zomato rider
identities are known via app login and device ID. Every other short-dwell device is classified as a non-Zomato rider —
giving KitchenPulse its first-ever real-time view of external kitchen load.

INSIGHT 2 — RIDER GPS GROUND TRUTH + SMALL RESTAURANTS
Eliminating Fast-Tapping with Real Timestamps
The rider's GPS captures arrival at the restaurant. The app captures the pickup scan time. The gap between these two
timestamps is the actual KPT — regardless of what the merchant fast-tapped as FOR. Data corruption becomes
irrelevant as a model input problem.
Three-Second Rider Feedback Prompt (Post-Pickup)
After pickup, rider sees one question: "Was the food ready when you arrived?" [ Yes ] [ No — I waited ] [ Partially ready ]
This builds a per-merchant bias correction profile — the model applies a merchant-specific adjustment factor to
future KPT predictions automatically.
Small & Mini Restaurants — Rider as Primary Signal (Zero Hardware)
Small dhabas, tiffin centres, and informal kitchens cannot afford IoT hardware. For these merchants, rider GPS
timestamps and the three-second prompt are the only data collection method needed — zero hardware, zero
merchant cooperation, zero customer action. Works from the very first Zomato order received.

INSIGHT 3 — TRUST SCORE SYSTEM
Replacing Penalties with a Reward for Honesty
The penalty system creates adversarial merchant behaviour. The Trust Score flips this — merchants who report
accurate KPT, even if genuinely slow, earn a higher score unlocking real platform benefits. This is a software-only
change scaling instantly to all 100,000+ merchants from day one at zero hardware cost.
Trust Score Formula
Trust Score = 100 - ( |KPT Error| x 3 ) + ( Merchant Rating x 5 ) [capped at 100]
A merchant accurately reporting a 35-minute KPT scores higher than one who fast-taps to appear fast but creates
rider wait times. Trust Score acts as a signal reliability weight when the model consumes FOR data.

INSIGHT 6 — SHARED COMPENSATION MODEL
When KPT Exceeds 2x ETA — Partners in Recovery
When a delay exceeds 2x the predicted ETA, automatic compensation triggers. The cost is shared between Zomato
and the merchant — because the prediction error is a shared responsibility. Neither party can be fully blamed, so
neither bears the full cost.

Why Shared Cost is the Right Design
The merchant's financial nudge is proportional and fair — not punitive. Repeated delays cost them a small item,
incentivising honest KPT without harsh penalties. When Zomato shares the cost it signals: we are partners in this, not
adversaries. This makes the Trust Score system credible and the whole incentive architecture coherent.
